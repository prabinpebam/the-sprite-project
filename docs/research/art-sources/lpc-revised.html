LPC Revised (ElizaWy)

A curated, restyled, and expanded reworking of the LPC art set - the art-direction backbone behind much of today's LPC content.

TL;DR

ElizaWy's LPC Revised project is a curated collection of Creative-Commons game assets in a bright ~170-color, 32-pixel style with a three-quarters top-down perspective (think Chrono Trigger / A Link to the Past). It spans Fantasy, Modern, and eventually Sci-Fi themes, uses larger-than-average sprites, and licenses everything CC-BY 3.0 or OGA-BY 3.0. At 191 stars it is the most-followed pure art source in this research.

What it offers

Characters

Larger sprites with more room for detail; the source of the "Revised" frame counts, palette, and smaller heads adopted by the generators.

World art

Terrain, Objects, Structure, and FX (including basic water ripples and splashes) - a fuller world-building kit, not just characters.

Palettes & guides

A shared palette and animation guides that the ULPC generator explicitly points contributors toward.

Its role in the ecosystem

The flagship generator credits ElizaWy with having "revised and expanded the LPC paradigm," and its own animation-frame guidance links directly to Eliza's animation guides. In the ecosystem's vocabulary, this repo is the origin of LPCR (Revised) and a driver of LPCE (Expanded).

Per-file attribution

Each asset ships with a credit.txt in the same directory naming the original author. Because everything is CC-BY 3.0 / OGA-BY 3.0, attribution is required but commercial use (including DRM platforms, under OGA-BY) is permitted.

Relevance to this project

LPC Revised is the art-direction reference: a coherent palette, perspective, and animation grammar. Any project aiming for stylistic consistency across a large sprite corpus should study how Eliza codified it - see the LPC Revised Style Guide for the exact palette, geometry, lighting, and material rules.
