Character Generators

Vitruvian LPC Studio

An alternative, modern-feeling LPC character generator app - paired with its own repository of converted spritesheets.

TL;DR

Vitruvian Studio (by napsio) is a second-generation LPC generator with a cleaner app-like UI, distinct Character and Horse modes, and import/export/save of builds. Its art lives in a companion spritesheets repo, reorganized into anatomy / clothes / equipment / horse / injuries. It is explicitly flagged as not yet production-ready, and the flagship ULPC generator lists it as an alternative.

App capabilities

Two subjects

Dedicated Character and Horse modes - animals are first-class, not an afterthought.

Animation set

Walk, Shoot, Slash, Backslash, Spell, Thrust, Rod, Whip, Hurt, and Idle, with zoom, speed, and per-frame controls.

Build persistence

Import, Export, and Save selections so a character can be reopened and iterated on later.

Reorganized art

The companion repo restructures LPC assets into anatomy, clothes, equipment, horse, and injuries categories.

The companion spritesheets repo

The vitruvianstudio/spritesheets repository holds the art the app consumes, colorized to default colors and licensed CC-BY-SA 3.0. It carries the standard LPC collective-credits block (dozens of contributing artists) and a machine-readable credits.json. Notably, the ULPC generator ships a convert-to-Vitruvian tool, so the two ecosystems are deliberately interoperable.

Maturity vs. the flagship

Vitruvian Studio

  • Cleaner, app-style interface; character + horse modes
  • Newer (2023), small following (5 stars on the asset repo)
  • Author's own note: "currently not suitable for productive use"

Universal LPC Generator

  • Feature-complete, license-aware exports, WebGL recolor
  • Mature, ~1,400 stars, actively maintained
  • Production-ready today

Relevance to this project

Vitruvian is the clearest example of a UX-forward rethink of LPC generation and of treating animals (horses) as first-class subjects. It signals appetite for a more polished, app-like sprite tool - and its interoperability tooling shows converting between asset layouts is expected.
