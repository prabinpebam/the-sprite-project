LPC Revised Style Guide

The written rulebook behind the LPC Revised look - the palette, geometry, lighting, and material conventions every asset must follow to stay compatible.

TL;DR

ElizaWy's Style Guide codifies how LPC Revised art is drawn: a fixed bright palette (required for the auto-recolor scripts), 32-pixel tiles, a top-down orthographic "three-quarter" perspective with strict pixel ratios, light from the top-front-left with a semi-transparent #2a1722 shadow, 1-pixel outlines, and per-material texturing rules. Sticking to it is what makes assets mix cleanly in the generators.

Perspective & scale

LPC and LPC Revised use a top-down orthographic perspective (a "three-quarter view"): objects are drawn without foreshortening, keeping full size regardless of distance - the classic video-game aesthetic. Concrete rules follow from that projection:

  • Diagonals: 1:2 pixel rise over run.
  • Squares on the Y and Z axis: 4:3 width-to-height. The top face of a perfect cube is 24px high and 32px wide.
  • Scale, X axis: 32px ≈ 3 feet / 90 cm (horizontal).
  • Scale, Y & Z axis: 24px ≈ 3 feet / 90 cm (vertical and depth).

Color palette

The project uses a fixed, bright, diverse palette stored in the Palette folder. This is not just an aesthetic preference - it is a technical contract.

The palette is enforced by tooling

LPC Revised uses automated scripts to recolor clothing and skin. Any color not in the approved ramps will not be recognized by those scripts - so off-palette pixels silently break recoloring. Assets must keep to the ramps.

Lighting & shadows

Light comes from the top, slightly to the front and left. Highlights fall on the top and left; shadows on the bottom and right. Shadows use a specific, semi-transparent color for consistent depth.

Outlines

Strong outlines

  • For characters and interactable objects
  • Solid, consistent 1-pixel thickness all the way around
  • Makes objects pop against the background

Soft outlines

  • For props, furniture, and soft-edged items
  • Lighter on the lit (top-front-left) portions
  • Color darker than the fill but lighter than the standard outline

Most assets carry a 1-pixel outline in a darker shade of the asset's primary color. Exceptions are allowed where a specific asset or aesthetic calls for it, but the rules are the default.

Material texture guidelines

Each common material has color and texture rules so surfaces read correctly at pixel scale.

MaterialColorTexture / notes
Wood Stay to the ramps Subtle horizontal/vertical grain; avoid harsh contrast; minimal or none for a clean look
Stone Varied grays; moss/dirt spots; uneven brown at the bottom to blend with terrain Uneven with some noise; smooth stone more uniform with slight noise
Metal Stick to the ramps Smooth unless old/weathered; high light-dark contrast for reflective metals
Glass Lighter tones, high contrast Smooth; 45° lines of varying thickness for reflection; alpha 120/256 when transparent
Fabric / Skin Keep to the ramps (required for auto-recolor) Preserve silhouette without busyness; silk smoother than cotton/wool; glossy = high contrast
Plants / Foliage Varying greens (reds/yellows/browns by plant or season) Texture reflects plant type (bark, veins, grass strands); add detail on larger plants; vary for a lively scene

Relevance to this project

This is the machine-and-human contract that keeps thousands of community assets visually and technically interoperable. Any sprite tool that wants to generate or validate LPC-compatible art should encode these exact constraints - palette membership, 32px grid, the 1:2 / 4:3 ratios, the #2a1722 shadow, and 1px outlines - as first-class rules.
